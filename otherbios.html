In-depth profiles of cryptids researched by the Cryptid Securement Foundation. For internal review and analysis only.

Common Name: Mothman

Date of First Sighting: November 15, 1966

Date of Death N/A

Kill Count: 46 (linked to Silver Bridge Collapse)

Description: A humanoid figure with large wings and glowing red eyes. Known for sightings in Point Pleasant, WV, and often associated with imminent disaster.

Common Name: Bigfoot

Date of First Sighting: 1958 (modern accounts)

Date of Death N/A

Kill Count: 0 (no verified kills)

Description: A large, ape-like creature with dense fur, primarily sighted in forested areas of North America. Known for elusive behavior and unconfirmed sightings.

Common Name: Wendigo

Date of First Sighting: Pre-colonial Native American lore

Date of Death N/A

Kill Count: Numerous (according to folklore)

Description: A mythological creature symbolizing insatiable hunger, said to embody cannibalistic spirits. Commonly described as skeletal, with sunken eyes and rotting flesh.

Common Name: Loch Ness Monster

Date of First Sighting: August 22, 565 AD (recorded by Saint Columba)

Date of Death N/A

Kill Count: Unknown (no confirmed incidents)

Description: Known as “Nessie,” an aquatic creature with a long neck and a hump-like back, reportedly seen in Loch Ness, Scotland. Sightings remain unverified.

Common Name: Jersey Devil

Date of First Sighting: 1735 (according to folklore)

Date of Death N/A

Kill Count: Numerous livestock (per local legends)

Description: A creature with a goat’s head, bat-like wings, hooves, and a long tail. Known for haunting the Pine Barrens of NJ and emitting a piercing scream.
